# Education, Income & Employment Trends Across Indian States (2001-2011)

## 📋 Project Overview
A data analytics project analyzing state-wise socio-economic data from India to identify literacy, income, unemployment, and poverty trends between 2001 and 2011 — providing insights for NGOs like **NayePankh Foundation** to guide education and youth empowerment programs.

## 🎯 Objective
To understand how literacy rates, income levels, unemployment, and poverty have changed across Indian states over a decade, and identify which states need the most educational and skill-development support.

## 📊 Dataset
- **Source:** RBI State-wise Socio-Economic Data
- **Coverage:** 32 Indian states and union territories
- **Time Period:** 2001 and 2011
- **Variables:** Income, Literacy Rate, Population, Sex Ratio, Unemployment Rate, Poverty Rate
- **Data Quality:** No missing values, no duplicates ✅

## 🔍 Key Findings

### 1. Literacy Growth (2001-2011)
- **Delhi** showed the highest literacy improvement (+18.61 points)
- Followed by **Bihar** (+14.80), **Uttarakhand** (+14.03), **Punjab** (+13.01), **Kerala** (+12.85)
- Every state improved — national education initiatives are working!

### 2. Income Growth (2001-2011)
- **Goa** recorded highest income growth (~₹1,67,835)
- Followed by **Delhi**, **Sikkim**, **Chandigarh**, **Haryana**

### 3. Literacy vs Unemployment (2011)
- High literacy doesn't always mean low unemployment
- **Goa** (87% literacy) had 51% unemployment
- **Gujarat** (86% literacy) had only 3% unemployment
- Conclusion: Skill development must accompany education

### 4. Poverty vs Literacy (2011)
- States with lower literacy generally had higher poverty
- **Chhattisgarh**: 70.28% literacy, 39.93% poverty (highest)
- **Bihar**: 61.80% literacy, 33.74% poverty

## 📈 Visualizations

## 💡 Recommendations for NayePankh Foundation
1. Prioritize education programs in **Bihar, Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand, Arunachal Pradesh, and Odisha**
2. Combine literacy programs with **vocational/skill training**
3. Focus on states with rising unemployment despite literacy gains (**Nagaland, Tripura**)
4. Use **Gujarat and Maharashtra** as success models for literacy + employment balance
